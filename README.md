This study presents a comprehensive diagnostic analysis of marine heatwave conditions in the Arabian Sea over the 2021–2025 period, based on 1,823 daily sea surface temperature fields from the NOAA OISSTv2.1 satellite dataset. The key findings are as follows:
 
●	Persistent heatwave state

●	Extreme 2023–2024 conditions

●	April as peak risk month

●	Erosion of monsoon cooling
 
These findings establish a quantitative baseline of recent Arabian Sea thermal conditions with direct relevance for coral reef management, fisheries assessment, cyclone risk evaluation, and monsoon research. The results are consistent with CMIP6 projections suggesting that mid-century MHW frequency levels (40–60%) for the Arabian Sea may already be occurring under current greenhouse gas forcing.
